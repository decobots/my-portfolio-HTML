About VV

VV is a well-known chain of eco-friendly goods in the central region of Russia. They have an excellent reputation as a mono-brand store. VV meticulously selects manufacturers, tests products, and seeks feedback, so customers are always sure about the items’ quality.

Problem

Unfortunately, it takes a lot of time and research to launch each new product, which limits VV’s catalog. And with limited catalog VV looses their online customers to other stores.

Solution

VV wants to add products from third-party brands in their online catalog. Having bigger variety to choose from might help to attract online customers.

State of UX and UI

VV already decided to present third-party items in new section of online catalog as a “Supermarket” based on marketing research.

They would like to have minimum additions to the existing Design System.

My task

To find a way to present new category and product cards in an easy to understand way.

My role

UX and UI design, with reviews from my mentor.

Approach

As an active customer of VV myself, I already had an idea of current user experience, so I focused on understanding why VV has problems with their existing catalog and how we can improve user experience.

Understand business goals

I know what VV tries to do, but I’m not sure why they do it this exact way. So I start from series of interviews with stackholders to understand goals and concerns.  To summarise all my findings I like to formulate Jobs to be done statements which easy to remember and quote:

I want to sell third-party products, so that customers can order everything in one place, because then they will choose VV more often

With a bigger variety of items there is a better chance that customers will be able to order all they need in VV

I want to sell more in each order, so customers will be eligible for free delivery and will choose VV more often

A bigger catalog leads to a bigger average check which allows free delivery to more customers

I want to sell third-party products, to attract new customers, so my revenue is bigger

With well known items in catalog VV might attract new categories of customers who weren’t interested in VV before

I want to find out which products are popular, so that I can release similar products under my own brand, because it brings-in more profit

Having sales data about outside products VV can make better decisions on launching their own brand’s items

How do others do it?

I am analyzing the strategies of businesses that sell products from various brands as well as products under their own brand. I found two approaches to their products: they either mix-in or they stand out from the third-party product.

Mix-in

Stores do not place a special mark indicating that the product is of their own brand. You can only guess based on the appearance and the name.

Image carousel

Stand out

Stores actively label products as their own brand, distinguishing them among products from other brands.

Image carousel

Conclusion

Companies that actively develop their brand image highlight their own products more frequently.

1st iteration

I’m starting from designing new elements for testing.

Image carousel

Testing findings

  • Users don't understand what “supermarket” is. Even if they know that VV is started to sell products from other brands.
  • After explaining the concept of a supermarket again, the difficulties disappear.
  • Respondents don't care who produced the item.
  • The banner seems too busy.

Fix it

Clarify "supermarket"

Since user's don't have problem understanding feature after explanation I add short onboarding animation and a note into supermarket category in the catalog.

"Busy" graphics

By toning down the gradient the new category looks more consistent with the rest of the app.

Is it better?

Second round of testing showed great improvement in user's understanding of the "supermarket" and better impression of UI.

But I found a new problem.

Users open the app not only for ordering products but also for scanning their loyalty cards in-store and checking deliveries.

Let's fix it

Before

With current flow users prefer to skip the onboarding because it's not why they came to the app at the moment. It’s not only defiese the purpose but angers users by distracting them from their original intention.
